HubSpot Connection

Two Questions.
Then We Connect.

OAuth and object mapping are on us. Two decisions need an ENTR answer — they shape how we map your data. Click each to expand.

2
Decisions Today
01
Decision · Needed today
Are all your customers in HubSpot, or just some?
We saw gaps in the migrated data — a handful of contracts in MN don't match a HubSpot company record cleanly. How we proceed depends on which scenario is true for ENTR.
+
Path A All customers should be in HubSpot
We map by external ID. Flag any MN contracts without a matching HubSpot Company. ENTR cleans up gaps in HS before we go live. Clean state, no orphans.
Path B Only some are in HubSpot
We map the customers that exist in HS. Remaining contracts live in MN as the source of record. Some customers won't have an associated Deal — that's expected.
Recommendation
Path A if ENTR uses HubSpot as the customer system of record. Path B if HubSpot is sales-pipeline-only and customer service lives elsewhere. We can also do hybrid: HS for active pipeline, MN as source of record for accounts.
02
Decision · Needed today
Map historical Deals to MN quotes, or start fresh?
Closed-won Deals already exist in HubSpot for past business. Question is whether we retrofit MN quotes onto them, or treat HubSpot history as reference-only and start clean going forward.
+
Path A Map historicals to MN
We create MN Quote records linked to historical Deals. Adds weeks of mapping work. Reporting becomes "complete" but the value is mostly cosmetic — historical Deals already closed.
Path B Clean cutover
Historical Deals stay in HubSpot as reference. From go-live forward, every new Deal creates a new MN Quote. Reporting works either way — past closed in HS, future originating in MN.
Recommendation
Clean cutover. Faster to live, less risk, no reconciliation. Historical Deal reporting is unchanged. Only your active contracts and renewals need accurate MN data — which we already migrated.
⚡
If you pick clean cutover: HubSpot connected and tested in sandbox within a few days of your confirmation. Production push follows once template config and approval rules are signed off.

Guardrails  ·  First 60 Days

Guardrails

The Five Things
That Tend to Go Sideways

Click any guardrail to see the symptom, root cause, and fix. Most issues come from these five places. Reps learn them in enablement, reinforce on go-live day.

5
Common Pitfalls
!
Wrong motion picked
Upsell booked as new business. Customer billed twice.
+
CauseRep created a new HubSpot Deal instead of amending the existing MN contract. Common when a rep is excited about a quick upsell and skips the contract.
FixAlways start amendments from the contract in MN, not from a new Deal. The "Amend" button on the contract creates the Deal automatically with correct linkage.
!
Wrong effective date
Proration math off. Customer pushes back on invoice.
+
CauseEffective date on the amendment quote left at default (today's date) instead of the actual change date negotiated with the customer.
FixConfirm effective date on every amendment before sending for signature. MN flags it during quote build. Rep validates against the customer agreement.
!
Stale primary quote
HubSpot Deal amount doesn't match what was billed.
+
CauseRep created multiple quote versions during negotiation. Closed-won on a non-primary version. Deal Amount and invoice don't reconcile.
FixMN flags the winning quote as primary on signature. Reps verify before close-won. UI shows "primary" badge on the signed quote.
!
Renewal not actioned
Customer reaches contract end without a renewal sent.
+
CauseRenewal Deal sat in HubSpot. Rep didn't see the Slack notification or didn't act. Contract lapses, customer churns or gets a gap.
FixSlack notification when window opens + 30-day reminder + 7-day escalation. Dashboard visibility in HubSpot showing renewals by stage with countdown.
!
QB customer mismatch
Invoice in MN doesn't land in QB cleanly.
+
CauseCustomer email or external ID doesn't match between MN and QB. Either a new QB customer record gets created (duplicate) or invoice fails to post.
FixCustomer ID mapping verified during QB connection setup. MN flags mismatches before pushing invoice. Reconciliation report runs weekly.
★
The rule of thumb
If it's a pricing or contract issue, fix it in MN. If it's pipeline, fix it in HubSpot. If it's ledger, fix it in QB. Each system owns its own truth — don't try to fix one in another.
